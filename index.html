Research
The Missing Twins: Transmission Channels Between Currency and Debt Crises Working paper, 2020

In this paper, I determine that currency crises help predict debt crises, and expand on existing literature investigating the role of foreign currency- denominated debt in currency and debt crises to propose some transmission channels that could help explain this association. Using a novel methodology combining a regression-based event study with a linear probability model, I find that foreign exchange reserve declines and tax revenue declines both (i) have a high likelihood of occurring following a currency crisis, and (ii) are associated with an increase in the likelihood of a debt crisis. Combining the coefficients from the models, I estimate that the fall in reserves associated with a currency crisis increases the likelihood of a debt crisis by up to 1.6%, while the decline in tax revenue associated with a currency crisis increases the likelihood of a debt crisis by up to 2.9%. These increases are non-trivial compared to the unconditional probability of a debt crisis, which is 17% in my sample. The empirical results thereby provide support for theories emphasizing the role of foreign exchange reserves and tax revenue in the transmission from a currency crisis to a debt crisis.
